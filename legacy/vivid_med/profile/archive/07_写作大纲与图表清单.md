### 1.1.1 Related Work（英文，paper-ready）

#### Visual instruction pretraining and top-down supervision for perception

Recent works have explored using language supervision—especially instruction-following objectives—to improve visual representations, arguing that high-level reasoning signals can shape low-level perception. A representative line is **visual instruction pretraining** that embeds a vision encoder into a VLM and optimizes next-token prediction on curated instruction-response data, then evaluates by extracting the pretrained vision backbone for downstream tasks (e.g., segmentation). While effective, these approaches primarily rely on **free-form textual responses** as supervision and often target improving the *overall* VLM or its instruction-following capability before transferring the vision encoder.

#### Foundation vision models for medical imaging

In medical imaging, foundation models are commonly learned via self-supervised or contrastive objectives (e.g., masked autoencoding, DINO-style pretraining) and then adapted to downstream classification/segmentation/regression. Another family trains vision-language models via large-scale image–text contrastive learning (e.g., CLIP variants in biomedicine), providing strong retrieval and zero-shot capabilities. However, medical deployment often prioritizes **robustness under domain shift**, **calibration**, and avoidance of **clinically critical errors** (e.g., laterality/measurement/unit mistakes), which are not explicitly controlled by most generic pretraining objectives.

#### Our positioning: verifiable semantic distillation for deployable medical ViTs

We differ from prior instruction-pretraining/VLM-centric approaches in both goal and supervision design. **Our goal is a deployable *ViT-only* medical backbone**, not an instruction-following VLM at inference. We treat a **frozen LLM as a semantic teacher manifold** and update only the vision encoder and a lightweight projector via **structured, verifiable token supervision**: outputs are constrained to a unified JSON schema, with **answerability-aware targets** (abstain when unanswerable) and a **programmatic verifier** that assigns training roles (positive/abstain/negative-only/drop) and enforces clinical unit/range constraints. This design explicitly addresses clinical reliability and enables evaluation beyond average accuracy—e.g., risk–coverage and hard-error rates—while retaining broad transferability in ViT-only downstream tasks under domain shift and low-label regimes.

> **One-sentence contrast to [ViTP]:** Unlike visual instruction pretraining that learns from free-form responses and improves a VLM before transferring its backbone, we perform **verifiable semantic distillation** where a frozen LLM provides a structured semantic supervision space to train a **deployable ViT-only** model with explicit answerability and clinical error control.

### 1.1.2 Related Work（中文，汇报/草稿版）

#### 视觉指令预训练与“自上而下”监督

近来的研究开始用语言监督（尤其是指令跟随训练）来反向塑造视觉表征，核心观点是“高层语义/推理信号可以改进底层感知”。典型做法是把 ViT 嵌入 VLM，通过指令-回答的 next-token 目标进行训练，然后在下游任务中抽取 ViT backbone 去做分割/检测等评测（如 ViTP）。这类方法证明了“语言信号→视觉表征”的可行性，但其监督形式多为**自由文本回答**，目标也更偏向先提升整体 VLM 的指令能力再迁移视觉编码器。

#### 医学影像基础模型与医学 VLM

医学影像领域更常见的是自监督/对比学习式预训练（MAE、DINO 等）或医学 CLIP 类图文对比学习（BiomedCLIP 等），再迁移到分类/分割/测量任务。尽管这些方法在平均指标上表现突出，但临床部署往往更关心：跨医院/机型的**域偏移鲁棒性**、**校准/可信度**、以及 laterality/单位/量纲等**临床硬错误**的控制，而这些在通用预训练目标里通常没有被显式建模。

#### 我们的方法定位：面向临床可靠性的“可验证语义蒸馏”

我们的目标不是训练一个推理时仍依赖 LLM 的 VLM，而是产出一个**可部署的 ViT-only 医学 backbone**。我们将冻结 LLM 视为包含医学概念关系的**语义监督空间（semantic manifold/teacher）**，仅更新 ViT+projector，并用**结构化可验证的 token 监督**训练：输出被约束为统一 JSON schema，引入字段级 answerability（不可判定则 abstain），并通过 programmatic verifier 将样本分流到 positive/abstain/negative-only/drop 等训练角色，同时校验单位/范围等临床约束。由此我们能在 ViT-only 下游迁移中提升跨域与低样本表现，并在 risk–coverage 与临床硬错误率等指标上体现更贴近临床的可靠性收益。

> **和 ViTP 的一句话差异**：ViTP 以自由文本回答做指令预训练、先优化 VLM 再迁移 ViT；我们以 **可验证结构化 token** 做语义蒸馏，冻结 LLM，只训练并交付 **ViT-only**，并显式建模 answerability 与临床硬错误控制。

### 1.1.3 Contribution bullets（英文版，可放 Introduction 末尾）

- **Verifiable Semantic Distillation.** We propose a training framework that distills a frozen LLM’s semantic manifold into a medical ViT by optimizing structured token prediction, updating only the vision encoder and a lightweight projector.
- **Answerability-aware learning.** We introduce field-level answerability targets and an abstention mechanism that is trained jointly and evaluated via selective prediction (risk–coverage).
- **Constraint-aware supervision with verifier-guided policy.** We enforce a unified JSON schema via constraint-aware teacher forcing and a programmatic verifier that assigns training roles (positive/abstain/negative-only/drop) and checks clinical constraints (units/ranges).
- **Clinically grounded evaluation.** We demonstrate improved ViT-only transfer under domain shift and low-label regimes, and report reliability metrics including calibration, risk–coverage, and clinically critical hard-error rates.

## 1.2 Introduction “三句话模板”（可直接改写进正文）

1. 现有医学视觉训练多依赖 one-hot/弱标签监督，难以显式编码医学概念的相似性与共现结构，且在跨域与干扰下容易出现 shortcut 与不可追责的错误。
2. 我们提出一种 **Semantic Knowledge Distillation** 范式：将冻结 LLM 视为包含医学概念关系的语义监督空间，通过受约束的结构化 JSON token 预测（UMS + verifier）训练 ViT 表征。
3. 训练后丢弃 LLM，仅保留 ViT，并在 external test / transfer / low-data 与可靠性指标（risk–coverage、clinical hard errors、anti-shortcut 硬测试）上验证其价值。

一句话类比（答辩/讨论好用）：
- **老师（LLM）只在训练时教，毕业后上岗的是学生（ViT）**；我们交付的是 ViT backbone，而不是一个推理时依赖 LLM 的系统。
